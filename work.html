I taught Physics for grades 10, 11, and 12 at SMAIT Permata Bunda for one year. I increased students’ interest in Physics by organizing more outdoor learning activities that connected physics concepts to everyday life.   • Physics Teacher

My teaching focused on strengthening students’ conceptual understanding and problem-solving abilities through interactive discussions, practice-based sessions, and real-life applications of physics and mathematics. To enhance students’ readiness for national university entrance exams, I developed and delivered structured lessons that emphasized analytical thinking and effective test-taking strategies. I also provided personalized guidance to help students identify their learning gaps, improve performance, and gain confidence in solving high-level problems.

  • DCC Global School
  • Physics Teacher

My teaching journey began at DCC Global School, an inclusive school where I first served professionally as a teacher. I taught Physics, General Science, and Music for students from grades 7 to 12 (junior to senior high school). Working in an inclusive learning environment taught me in practice—not just in theory—that every student deserves equal access to quality education. As a homeroom teacher for Grade 7, I supported students’ academic and personal growth through consistent communication and guidance. In both Physics and Music classes, I emphasized hands-on learning by designing experiments and creative projects that allowed students to explore concepts meaningfully. These experiences helped me develop a student-centered approach that values curiosity, creativity, and inclusivity in every learning process.
