Social Projects

Rumah Pintar

Rumah Pintar is a social initiative established in Hadiluwih Village, Punggur District, Central Lampung Regency, Lampung Province. This project began from our concern about elementary school children who had no academic activities outside of school. With limited study hours and parents who mostly worked in the fields, these children lacked structured learning support at home. To address this, we organized supplementary learning sessions (tutoring) held three times a week at an affordable cost of only Rp4,000 per session. Through this simple system, the children not only gained additional learning opportunities but also learned to save and spend their money for meaningful educational purposes. All collected funds were used to support the learning process—such as printing exercises, buying markers, and providing a whiteboard. This activity became a fun and meaningful educational space for local students, while strengthening the sense of community in the village. Through this project, I learned that education doesn’t always need to start from big institutions—it can grow from a small intention to share knowledge. I also discovered how powerful community collaboration can be in nurturing the spirit of learning, and how valuable it is to teach responsibility and independence from an early age.

Balikk (L)ampung

Balikklampung is a community movement that encourages young people from Lampung to contribute to their homeland. Living in an asrama environment, members engage in both physical and spiritual development, alongside social initiatives that support local communities. The community nurtures collaboration, empathy, and leadership through activities such as volunteering, teaching, and environmental projects. Through this experience, I learned the essence of giving back—using knowledge and service to create meaningful impact, while growing in discipline, teamwork, and purpose.
